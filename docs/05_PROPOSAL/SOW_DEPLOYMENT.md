## 1. Project Objectives

The objectives of this engagement are to:

1. Deploy GovFlow as a secure, governance-ready workflow platform for the Client.
2. Enable standardized digital workflows, approvals, and reporting across identified departments/entities.
3. Implement the agreed deployment model (default: Hybrid for UAE public sector unless otherwise approved).
4. Establish operational readiness, support model, and acceptance for production use.
5. Provide knowledge transfer to ensure controlled ongoing operations by Client and/or SW Solutions support teams.

---

## 2. Scope of Services (Included)

SW Solutions shall provide the following services within this SOW:

### 2.1 Program Initiation and Governance
- Project kickoff, governance structure, and communication plan
- Confirmation of scope boundaries, success criteria, and delivery milestones
- Risk and dependency register initiation

### 2.2 Solution Design and Environment Planning
- Target architecture confirmation (cloud/hybrid/on-prem as contracted)
- Environment model definition (Dev, Staging, UAT, Production)
- Security and compliance baseline planning aligned to Client policy requirements

### 2.3 Platform Deployment and Configuration
- GovFlow platform installation and base configuration
- Identity and access setup (role model and federation alignment as required)
- Workflow and approval configuration per agreed requirement set
- Baseline white-label configuration (branding/domain/profile)

### 2.4 Integration and Data Enablement
- Configuration of agreed standard integrations (as listed in contract annex)
- Data setup and migration support for agreed in-scope datasets
- Validation of configured interfaces and data flows

### 2.5 Testing, UAT, and Go-Live
- Support for system/integration testing cycles
- UAT support and issue resolution for in-scope defects
- Production readiness checks and controlled go-live support

### 2.6 Hypercare and Handover
- Post go-live stabilization support for agreed hypercare period
- Operational handover package (runbook, admin guidance, support pathways)
- Closure report and formal sign-off support

## 6. Assumptions

This SOW is based on the following assumptions:

1. Client provides required access, environments, and dependencies on schedule.
2. Client stakeholders are available for timely reviews, decisions, and UAT participation.
3. Scope changes are managed through formal change control.
4. Required third-party licenses/services are procured by the responsible party as defined in contract annexes.
5. Regulatory, legal, and internal policy approvals are obtained by the Client in required timelines.

---

## 7. Out of Scope

Unless explicitly included in a signed change order or annex, the following are out of scope:

- Custom module development beyond agreed requirements
- New integrations not listed in the approved scope baseline
- Third-party licensing/subscription fees and procurement management
- Client internal infrastructure procurement and datacenter operations
- Extended organizational change management programs beyond agreed training scope
- Ongoing managed services beyond agreed hypercare/support period

---

## 8. Acceptance Criteria

The project (or each phase) shall be considered accepted when all applicable criteria below are met:

1. **Deliverables Completed:** All in-scope deliverables for the phase are submitted.
2. **Functional Conformance:** Agreed business workflows and configurations are operational in target environments.
3. **Testing Completion:** Defined SIT/UAT cycles completed with accepted defect closure threshold.
4. **Security/Compliance Baseline:** Required controls/checklists completed per agreed policy baseline.
5. **Operational Readiness:** Runbooks, support model, and escalation paths are documented and approved.
6. **Formal Sign-Off:** Client authorized representative provides written acceptance.

If acceptance criteria are not met, both parties will agree a remediation plan with timeline.

---

## 9. Change Control

Any requested change to scope, timeline, or deliverables shall be managed through formal change request (CR) procedure, including:
- Impact assessment (scope, cost, schedule, risk)
- Approval by authorized representatives of both parties
- Updated baseline documentation

No change shall be considered binding without written approval.
